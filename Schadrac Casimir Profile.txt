Schadrac Casimir scored over 1,500 points at the Division I level despite standing just 5’8”. He first made his mark at Trinity Catholic High School, where he averaged 27 points per game as a senior, earned All-State honors three times, was named Player of the Year, and helped lead his team to a state championship during his sophomore season.



After Trinity Catholic, Casimir attended South Kent School, competing in the highly competitive NEPSAC league. There he averaged 19 points per game and recorded a season-high 37 points against Brewster Academy, a team led by future NBA star Donovan Mitchell.



Casimir went on to play Division I basketball at Iona College, where he was named 2015 Freshman All-American and MAAC Rookie of the Year. During his freshman season he made 94 three-pointers, ranking second among all freshmen in the country, just behind D’Angelo Russell. He also recorded a 40-point performance against Delaware State. While at Iona, he helped lead the program to three NCAA Tournament appearances, competing against top programs including Duke and Oregon.



He finished his collegiate career at Florida Gulf Coast University, where he averaged a team-high 14 points per game and earned Second Team All-ASUN honors.



Today, Casimir uses his experience competing at the highest levels of college basketball to train and mentor the next generation of players through Casimir Basketball Development, focusing on skill development, basketball IQ, and the work ethic required to succeed.
