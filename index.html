About the Coach

Trained by Someone
Who's Been There

Schadrac Casimir scored over 1,500 points at the Division I level despite standing just 5'8". He first made his mark at Trinity Catholic High School, where he averaged 27 points per game as a senior, earned All-State honors three times, was named Player of the Year, and helped lead his team to a state championship during his sophomore season. He set the Connecticut record for points in a season with 722 and scored a 50-point game against Greenwich.

After competing in the elite NEPSAC league at South Kent School—where he scored 36 points against a Brewster Academy team led by future NBA star Donovan Mitchell—Casimir went on to star at Iona College, earning 2015 Freshman All-American honors and MAAC Rookie of the Year. He was a three-time MAAC Conference Champion and helped lead Iona to two NCAA Tournament appearances, competing against top programs including Duke and Oregon.

He finished his collegiate career at Florida Gulf Coast University, averaging a team-high 14 PPG and earning Second Team All-ASUN honors. He broke the school record for free throw percentage (89%) and three-point percentage (47%), and led the country in three-point percentage for half of the season. Today, Casimir uses his experience to train and mentor the next generation through Casimir Basketball Development.

The Journey

A Path Forged at
the Highest Level

From Connecticut high school legend to Division I standout, Coach Casimir's journey is the foundation of his training philosophy.

High School

Trinity Catholic High School

State Champion as a sophomore. Three-time All-State selection and Player of the Year as a senior. Averaged 27 PPG his senior season, scored a 50-point game vs Greenwich, and set the Connecticut record for points in a season with 722.

Prep School

South Kent School (NEPSAC)

Competed in one of the nation's toughest prep leagues. Scored 36 points against Brewster Academy (led by future NBA star Donovan Mitchell), 29 points against St. Thomas More (led by Eric Paschall), and 36 points against Northfield Mount Hermon. Second Team All-NEPSAC.

College — Iona

Iona College (MAAC)

2015 Freshman All-American and MAAC Rookie of the Year. Hit 94 three-pointers as a freshman (2nd nationally, just behind D'Angelo Russell). 40-point game vs Delaware State. All-Conference selection. Three-time MAAC Conference Champion. Two NCAA Tournament appearances, competing against Duke and Oregon.

College — FGCU

Florida Gulf Coast University (ASUN)

Averaged a team-high 14 PPG. Second Team All-ASUN. Broke the school record for free throw percentage (89%) and three-point percentage (47%). Led the country in three-point percentage for half of the season. Surpassed 1,500 career D1 points.

Today

Casimir Basketball Development

Using his experience competing at the highest levels of college basketball to train and mentor the next generation of players. Focused on skill development, basketball IQ, and the work ethic required to succeed.
